Messaging

This visualization is an interactive slideshow which talks about the population of top 5 most populated countries of the world in 2018 (China, India, USA, Indonesia & Pakistan). I have looked at the WDI dataset. I have presented a population growth trend for these countries in last 60 years. Once we see the trend for population growth, the next scene depicts the corresponding population growth in rural & urban population for these countries. This will give user an idea that the country like India where the population is increasing for both rural & urban and would surpass China (most populated country in 2018). The population growth trend is always on a rise for urban areas for all the countries,hinting that because of the available opportunities rural population is moving towards cities.

Narrative Structure

This visualization follows the "Interactive Slide Show" structure. The user is given the visual information about the data based on the scenes depicted in the story. The user has the option to drilldown in the details (mouse over the line charts to view & compare the population of the countries for a particular year, filter the data for the "urban" & "rural" population growth per country) or move on to the next scene. This visualization tends to focus more on stronger messaging rather than free form exploration.

Visual Structure

Structure - The data is presented to the user in a simple form that present the information clearly whether it is depicting quantitative & nominal data with bar charts or quantitative & quantitative data with line charts, clear use of labels/text for frames to communicate the details

Highlight - Important information that I want to present is highighted using the annotations

Transition - I have made sure that the user is not disoriented from scene to scene. I have used the same dimension for the svg canvas, the contours used to depict different countries. In case of similar information presented in different scenes (rural & urban growth), I used similar scale for axis to depict the growth in rural population is negative in some cases as compared to urban population growth that is always positive.

Scenes

I have developed the scenes ars the slides in the presentation and added the following scenes.

  • Scene 1- To depict the population for top 5 most populated countries of the world
  • Scene 2- This scene presents the population growth for last six decades for these 5 countries.
  • Scene 3- Once user get an idea for the overall growth trend, I present the growth trend for rural & urban population.

The scenes are ordered to tell the story about the population growth for few countries & answer the questions that user might have, looking at scene 1, scene 2 & scene 3 respectively. The user will come to conclusion that the country where there is increase in both rural & urban population like India is on the path to be the most populated country.

Annotations

I have used a text to convey the important message for the scene. The other way of communicating the annotations used are clear labels for the axis and tooltip is used in few scenes to provide the detail on demand information. This will help user to get a high level message from the scene. The annotations change from scene to scene but not within a scene.


Parameters

Parameters allows user to transition from one view to another. In the case of rural and urban population scene, I have provided the parameter with the legends (Country) at the bottom of the chart. Each legend will result in a different state of the scene.


Triggers

Triggers are used to interact with parameters and change the state of the scene. In our visualization story, the trigger is click of the legend (Country) in rural and urban population growth scene to filter the data for different countries. I have added them at the bottom of the chart unlike the previous scenes to attract user's attention. I still used the same colors to make sure user is not disoriented in the transition.
